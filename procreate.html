Procreate Collaboration

Introducing collaboration and communication tools to enhance Procreate's digital illustration process

Role
UX Researcher, UX/UI Designer

Tools
Figma, Google Drive

Work
User Research, User Testing, UI Design, UX Writing

Team
Rhiannon Lang, Elina Shankar, Janet Wong

Duration
10 weeks (January to March 2022)

Overview

Procreate is a popular iPad art application, widely used by professional and recreational artists. Yet, it currently lacks streamlined features for collaboration.

We aimed to improve Procreateâ€™s collaboration and communication process by making it more efficient to do collaborative tasks such as drawing simultaneously, sharing pieces, and making feedback-based edits.

Research

Methods
  • Released Google Forms survey to online art communities
  • Conducted 8 user interviews for qualitiative insight

Findings

1. Collaboration is a tedious and sequential process.

Artists take extra steps to share and download files with others, relying on external apps (Dropbox, Google Drive) to do the job.


2. Lack of within-file communication and demonstration methods.

In-depth feedback and comments cannot be directly provided on a file. Hand-drawn markups to suggest or indicate changes is not a specific or obvious feature in Procreate either.


3. Online collaboration is "more desirable" due to remote work.

Online educational and professional art settings that use Procreate need methods optimized for easy learning, communication, and collaboration.

(Procreate users requesting collaboration features)
